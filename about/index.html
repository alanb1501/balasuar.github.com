About

Nov 1st, 2012

Hello world.

My name is Alan, and this is my blog.

I’m VP of Engineering for a startup in Seattle, and I’ve been a developer for over 10 years. My experiences as a developer are full-stack. I’ve done work in real-time embedded systems, high-performance trading systems, desktop application, security servers, web applications, high scalable cloud based SaaS systems, and most recently mobile apps. Now I’m delving into social, building a platform that allows content creators to better engage with their fans socially.

Coding is my biggest passion. I’m pretty good at it, but more importantly I love it. I spend hours at the office writing software, and when I get home, I’m behind the keyboard writing more code.

You can find me on stackoverflow answering questions from time to time. My specific skillset is a strong mix of: C++, C#, HTML5, JS, Python, Perl, and Objective-C, along with various frameworks and operating systems.

My favorite language is Javascript, with C# being a very close 2nd. Python was once my all-time favorite language, but it’s changed so much since I first learned it, that I no longer get as much joy out of using it.

I’m a tinkerer by heart. I wouldn’t classify myself as a hacker–knowing the skills of real hackers, I find it disingenous to call myself one. However, when people colloquially talk about “hackers” I generally fit that profile.

This blog is powered by Octopress. It’s a blog engine that emphasizes code and scripts to generate content. It’ built ontop of git, which is my current favorite VCS. I’m typing this blog in Markdown, using Sublime Text 2 on my Macbook Pro. My previous blog was a wordpress based blog, which was fine, but really had too much going which detracted from the experience.

Outside of coding, I’m a gadget head. I’m well versed in home theater tech, computers (that goes without saying), and for a time I was into power tools.

Leaving tech, I’m an avid photographer. My online gallery has some of my work. It’s in a need of an overhaul, but that’s a weekend project for another time. I enjoy shooting scenery shots, so I prefer to use a wide-angle lens. I primarily shoot with my Canon 5D Mark II, and my goto lens is the 16-35mm F2.8 L Series II. 16mm on a full frame body allows for some stunning indoor shots.

Along with Photography, I love to travel. I’ve been to a number of places in my life, and while running engineering for a startup pretty much consumes all my time, I can’t wait until I’m able to jump back into traveling.

Of the amazing places I’ve visited, Turkey is my favorite. The people are amazing, it has a rich world history, the food is fantastic, and the scenery is breathtaking. I seriously was amazed at how beautiful my time over there was.

I have a varied background in martial arts as well. Most recently I’ve been training heavily in Krav Maga, a non-competitive martial art that emphasizes offense and aggression. I’m now apprenticing as an instructor as well, helping train new students in the basics of Krav Maga. In addition to KM, I have experience in submission wrestling, muay-thai, boxing, Tae Kwon Do, and basic wrestling. All of which is incorporated with my Krav training.

So developer, entraprenuer, traveler, face-puncher, photographer. That pretty much describes me.
